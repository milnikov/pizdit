# Source Quality Rules

Source priority, from strongest to weakest:

1. Election commission data
2. Government records
3. Parliamentary, municipal, or legislative voting records
4. Official candidate programs
5. Official party programs
6. Public statistics databases
7. Court records
8. Reputable media
9. Independent fact-checking organizations
10. Academic or NGO reports
11. Verified social media accounts of candidates or parties
12. Other sources

Weak sources must not be used as primary evidence unless clearly marked.

Weak sources include:
- Anonymous blogs
- Partisan channels without evidence
- Unsourced screenshots
- Edited clips without full context
- Anonymous social media posts
- Campaign attack ads without supporting documentation
